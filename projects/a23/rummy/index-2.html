A23 Rummy

Simplifying Complexity to Drive Retention & Trust

Overview

I led the end-to-end redesign of critical user journeys within A23 Rummy, focusing on improving retention, usability, and trust in a high-stakes gaming environment.

Role

Lead UX Designer

Timeline

Dec 2022 - Apr 2023

Overview

I led the end-to-end redesign of critical user journeys within A23 Rummy, focusing on improving retention, usability, and trust in a high-stakes gaming environment.

Project details - Rummy lobby revamp

Owned the UX strategy across onboarding, lobby, and gameplay entry. Mentored and guided 1 junior designer. Collaborated with Product Managers, Business stakeholders, and Compliance teams. Balanced UX improvements with regulatory and revenue constraints.

The Challenge

A23 Rummy was experiencing low user retention and friction in core gameplay entry flows, driven by three key issues:

Cognitive Overload

Too many game types and betting options caused users to struggle with quick decisions, leading to drop-offs.

Fragmented Onboarding

Multiple quick fixes over time led to a bloated flow, further complicated by government regulatory constraints.

High Risk of Costly Misclicks

Users frequently selected incorrect bet amounts, resulting in financial anxiety and reduced trust in the platform.

Strategy & Ideation

Reduce cognitive load while increasing trust and control.

Simplification Without Limiting Choice

Reorganized game types and bet values into structured, scannable formats prioritizing progressive disclosure.

Transparency as a Trust Lever

Clearly communicated bonus vs cash usage and reduced ambiguity in real-money interactions.

Safer Gameplay Design

Introduced safeguards to prevent accidental high-value bets and encourage responsible gameplay.

Dual Optimization Strategy

Balanced clarity for new users with speed, familiarity, and minimal disruption for VIP users.

My Approach

Streamlined previously fragmented flows, removing unnecessary steps from past quick fixes while ensuring strict regulatory compliance. Structured game types and bet amounts into clear, hierarchical layouts to reduce visual clutter and help users make confident decisions. Redesigned interactions to minimize accidental taps and provide clearer differentiation between bet tiers.

Personas - High Roller, Casual Player, New commer

Understanding our diverse user base was critical. We identified three primary personas to guide our design decisions:

Persona Behavior Needs Pain Points
High Roller Plays high stakes, frequent sessions Speed, familiarity, quick re-buy Drastic UI changes, slow animations
Casual Player Plays mid-stakes, weekend warrior Clear game rules, easy navigation Cluttered lobby, confusing bet amounts
New Comer First time player, learning the ropes Guidance, safe environment, tutorials Cognitive overload, fear of losing money

Measured Impact

By simplifying decision-making and increasing transparency, the platform achieved higher user confidence in gameplay, reduced friction in core flows, and stronger retention across user segments.

75% D1 Retention
38% D7 Retention
11% D30 Retention
+20% Games Played
+40% New User Funnel

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of designing for high-stakes environments where mistakes have real consequences. It was a masterclass in balancing business goals, user trust, and complex regulatory constraints while scaling experiences for both novice and expert users.
